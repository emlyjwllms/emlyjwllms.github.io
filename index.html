I am a Department of Energy Computational Science Graduate Fellow at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in Aeronautics and Astronautics with a specialization in Aerospace Computational Engineering. I completed my undergraduate degree in Aerospace Engineering with a minor in Atmospheric Sciences and a concentration in Computational Science and Engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in May 2021. I completed my master's thesis on assessing wall-modeled large-eddy simulation for high-speed flows and exploring new modeling strategies in the Computational Turbulence Group advised by Adrian Lozano-Duran. The numerical schlieren visualizations below show the pressure gradient contours and surface pressure percentages on the supersonic aircraft, the Lockheed Martin X-59 QueSST, which was a main focus of my thesis. For my PhD, I will investigate stochastic integrators for forward analysis of linearized chaotic systems and potential connections to subgrid-scale modeling in the Aerospace Computational Science and Engineering Laboratory advised by Dave Darmofal. My ultimate goal is to employ novel approaches to model multi-scale chaotic dynamical systems.
