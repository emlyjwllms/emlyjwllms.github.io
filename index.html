I am a Department of Energy Computational Science Graduate Fellow at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in Aeronautics and Astronautics with a specialization in Aerospace Computational Engineering. I am working on physics-based and data-driven modeling for computational fluid dynamics of supersonic aircraft in the Computational Turbulence Group. I completed my undergraduate degree in Aerospace Engineering with a minor in Atmospheric Sciences and a concentration in Computational Science and Engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in May 2021. My goal is to employ novel approaches to model multi-scale chaotic dynamical systems.

I am currently working on using information theory and machine learning to improve subgrid-scale modeling for wall-modeled large-eddy simulation for supersonic external aerodynamic applications, such as the X-59 Quiet SuperSonic Technology (QueSST) being developed by Lockheed Martin and NASA.
