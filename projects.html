FD Solver for 1D Linear Euler Equations

AE 410: Computational Aerodynamics, UIUC (2021)

To derive the 1D linear acoustic equations in $p$ and $\rho$, we start from the linearized Euler equations, neglecting the energy equation: \begin{align} \frac{\partial p}{\partial t} + K_0 \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} &= 0 \\ \bar{\rho} \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} + \frac{\partial p}{\partial x} &= 0 \end{align} where $K_0(x) = \bar{\rho}(x)\bar{p}(x)$ is the bulk modulus of compressibility of the gas, where $\bar{\rho}(x)$ and $\bar{p}(x)$ are the flow background density and pressure, and $p(x,t)$ and $u(x,t)$ are the unknowns of the problem. We consider a fluid in a channel of unit width and assume that the background velocity of the flow is negligible ($\bar{u}=0$). We wish to study the equations of linear acoustics in a layered medium in which $\bar{\rho}$ and $K_0$ are piecewise constant functions of $x$. We define the speed of sound $c(x)$ and the impedance $Z(x)$ as: \begin{align} c(x) &= \sqrt{\frac{K_0(x)}{\bar{\rho}(x)}} = \begin{cases} c_l, & \text{if }x \leq a\\ c_c, & \text{if }x\in(a,b) \\ c_r, & \text{if }x\geq b \\ \end{cases}\\ Z(x) &= \bar{\rho}(x)c(x) = \begin{cases} K_l, & \text{if }x \leq a\\ K_c, & \text{if }x\in(a,b) \\ K_r, & \text{if }x\geq b \\ \end{cases} \end{align} A finite-difference solver was developed to solve the linear acoustic equations on a finite domain $x \in [-5,5]$. The boundary at $x=5$ models a solid wall ($u(5,t)=0$), while at $x=-5$, a non-reflecting boundary condition is imposed such that the amplitude of the incoming characteristic is zero. The initial conditions were given as: \begin{align} p(x,0) &= 2 \text{exp} (-(x+3.0)^2/0.6^2) \\ u(x,0) &= 2 \text{exp} (-(x+3.0)^2/0.6^2) \end{align} The equations were integrated for at least 10 seconds using 151 points in the $x$-direction. The system of equations were rearranged in the following form: \begin{align} \frac{\partial q}{\partial t} + A\frac{\partial q}{\partial x} = 0 \end{align} where $q = [p,u]^T$ and $A$ = $[$ $0$ $K_0$ ; $1/\bar{\rho}$ $0$ $]$ = $[$ $0$ $cZ$ ; $c/Z$ $0$ $]$. Eigenvalues of A were determine to represent the speed of sound $c(x)$. Using the characteristic variables, boundary conditions that needed to be applied at $x=5$ to achieve a solid wall and $x=-5$ to achieve a non-reflection condition were determined by defining $w = X^{-1}q$ and $\Lambda = X^{-1}AX$: \begin{align} X^{-1} [\frac{\partial q}{\partial t} + A\frac{\partial q}{\partial x} &= 0] \\ \frac{\partial X^{-1} q}{\partial t} + \frac{\partial X^{-1} Aq}{\partial x} &= 0 \\ \frac{\partial X^{-1} q}{\partial t} + \frac{\partial X^{-1} A XX^{-1}q}{\partial x} &= 0 \\ \frac{\partial X^{-1} q}{\partial t} + \frac{\partial \Lambda X^{-1} q}{\partial x} &= 0 \\ \frac{\partial w}{\partial t} + \Lambda\frac{\partial w}{\partial x} &= 0 \end{align} The specular reflection at the right boundary is defined as $\frac{\partial u}{\partial t}(x=5) = \frac{\partial w_2}{\partial t}(x=5) = 0$. The non-reflection at the left boundary is enforced by prescribing $\frac{\partial w}{\partial t}(x=-5) = 0$.

Three FD schemes were implemented: 2nd-order central, 3rd-order biased, and 6th-order Padé. The equations were integrated and then pressure and velocity profiles at different times are captured.
1D Linear and Nonlinear Advection

ATMS 502/CSE 566: Numerical Fluid Dynamics, UIUC (2021)

For this project, we needed to solve the 1D advection (transport) equation for a variable $q$ in linear and nonlinear cases: \begin{align} \text{Linear: } \frac{\partial q}{\partial t} &= -c \frac{\partial q}{\partial x} \\ \text{Nonlinear: } \frac{\partial q}{\partial t} &= -q \frac{\partial q}{\partial x} \end{align} The solution domain is a 1D mesh (grid) of $nx$ points. The PDEs above are solved for each point on this mesh. The initial condition is a single sine wave with a wavelength of $nx\Delta x$ with a periodic boundary condition (i.e. the wave "exits" on the right side of the domain and "re-enters" on the left side for speed $c > 0$). The Lax-Wendroff scheme with forward time differencing and centered space differencing is employed to solve these PDEs: \begin{align} q_j^{n+1} = q_j^n - \frac{\nu}{2}(q_{j+1}^n - q_{j-1}^n)\\ + \sigma(q_{j+1}^n - 2q_j^n + q_{j-1}^n) \end{align} where $q$ is the scalar field that is being advected, $n$ is the time level, $j$ is indexing the $nx$ grid points, $\nu$ is the Courant number, and $\sigma = \nu^2/2$. For the linear case, $\nu = c\Delta t/\Delta x$, and $q_j^n \Delta t/\Delta x$ otherwise. Three cases were implemented in C:

Compressible Boundary Layer over Flat Plate

AE 498: Hypersonic Aerothermodynamics, UIUC (2020)

For this project, we integrated a compressible boundary layer over a flat plate with zero pressure gradient, under the assumption of calorically perfect gas. The governing equations are: \begin{align} & (Cf'')' + ff' = 0 \\ & (Cg') + \text{Pr} fg' + \text{Pr} C(\gamma - 1) \text{M}_e^2f''^2 = 0 \end{align}

where $f'(\eta) = u/V_e$ and $g(\eta) = T/T_e$. These equations are subject to the following boundary conditions:

\begin{align} & \text{Wall: } f(0) = f'(0) = 0 \text{, } g(0) = g_w \text{ or } g'(0) = 0 \\ & \text{Edge: } f'(\infty) = g(\infty) = 1 \end{align} The solution procedure is to use a shooting technique:
  1. make a first guess for $f''(0)$ and $g'(0)$
  2. integrate the governing equations across the boundary layer to values of $\eta$ large enough that $f'(\eta)$ and $g(\eta)$ are constant (up to the outer flow)
  3. verify that the resulting values of $f'(\eta)$ and $g(\eta)$ satisfy the freestream boundary conditions on the edge
  4. repeat the process adjusting the guesses until convergence is achieved
The state vector of unknows is $F = (f,f',f'',g,g')$. As input parameters, we need to define $\text{M}_e, \text{Pr}, \gamma$ and an expression for the Chapman-Rubesin parameter $C = (\rho\mu)/(\rho_e \mu_e)$. Using a power law of exponent $\sigma$ for the viscosity $\mu/\mu_e = (T/T_e)^\sigma$. Using the ideal gas law, we have $C = (\rho\mu)/(\rho_e \mu_e) = g^{\sigma - 1}$. The governing equations can be rewritten: \begin{align} & f''' = -\frac{f''}{g^{\sigma-1}}[(\sigma-1)g^{\sigma-2}g' + f] \\ & g'' = -\frac{1}{g^{\sigma-1}}[(\sigma-1)g^{\sigma-2}g'^2 + \text{Pr}fg'\\& + \text{Pr}g^{\sigma-1}(\gamma-1)\text{M}_e^2f''^2] = 0 \end{align} We assumed $\sigma = 0.75, \text{Pr} = 0.72, \text{ and } \gamma = 1.4$. We considered a boundary layer with the following edge conditions: $\text{M}_e = 5$, $\text{Re}_{e,x} = 10,000$, $T_e = 223 \text{ K}$, $P_e = 1000 \text{ Pa}$, $\mu_e = 1.2 \times 10^{-5}$ Pa-s. Skin friction coefficients $C_f$ and Stanton numbers $\text{ St }$ were computed for the three cases analyzed: \begin{align} & C_f = \sqrt{2}C_w\frac{f''(0)}{\sqrt{\text{Re}_{e,x}}} \\ & \text{St } = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\frac{C_w}{\text{Pr}}\frac{T_e}{T_{aw}-T_w}\frac{g'(0)}{\sqrt{\text{Re}_{e,x}}} \end{align} where $C_w = g_w^{\sigma-1}$ and $T_{aw} = T_e + \sqrt{\text{Pr}}(T_{0e}-T_e)$.
